Lung Meditation

Let's begin by tracing the Lung meridian. Start by placing your both hands at your belly, move up to the chest, through the lungs and toward both sides of the neck to the clavicle. Now down the inner arm passing the wrist towards the thumb.

As you meditate, concentrate on tracing this path focusing on the words in bold. The color you should visualize is white, element is metal, taste is pungent, think of weightlessness or falling softly like a feather.

Large Intestine Meditation

Starts from tip of forefinger and runs up the forefinger between the forefinger and thumb. Up the arm to elbow, then up the upper arm to highest part of the shoulders. One branch goes to the lungs then down to the Li. Another goes up along the neck to the face, moving across the lips to the opposite side of the nose

As you meditate, concentrate on tracing this path focusing on the words in bold. The color you should visualize is letting go and releasing at every exhale along the meridian. white, element is metal, taste is pungent, think of walking along an open field.

Stomach Meridian Medidation

Starting near the sides of the nose, traveling up to the eyes then down the jaw. One branch goes up the side of the face to the corner of the forehead. Another goes down the neck to the front of the body, first to the diaphram then the stomach. It continues down the front of the leg to the knees, then all the way to the first 3 toes.

Imagine something yellow, fragrant, and letting go of thoughts as you follow the course of the meridian down the body and releasing through the first 3 toes.

 

Heart Meridian Medidation

Beginning at the heart, one branch goes down the diaphram to the small intesting, another runs up the chest, up the neck to the eyes. The last branch goes across the chest, through the lungs and to the armpit. From there it travels down the inner armto the palms, ending at the pinky. 

As you meditate, imagine filling your heart with overwhelming joy as your concentrate on spreading joy through the pathway of the heart meridian, across the torso and arms, all the way down to the pinky. The color is pink and red.

Spleen Meridian Medidation

Beginning at the big toeand traveling up the inner foot, then to the inner leg and the groin area where it continues to the abdomen, internally to the stomach and spleen. It continues to the throat to the root of the tongue. Another branch travels down to the heart. 

As you meditate on this path, connect the inner legs, the stomach, up to the chest, throat and tongue. The taste is sweet, the color is yellow and imagine traveling through a marsh as  you pull your legs out of the marsh, lifting the energy up. 

Small Intestine Meridian Medidation

This meridian begins at the pinky and travels up the outer edge of the arm all the up to the shoulder. From here, a branch descends through the chest, through the heart, stomach then small intestine. Another branch ascends from the shoulder to the neck and up to the cheeks where it continues to the outer eye and ear.  

The color is red. Imagine traveling through a city with confidence and discernment.

Kidney Meridian Medidation

This meridian begins at the small toe, moving to the bottom of the foot then towards the ankle, traveling up the inner legand enters the body at the base of the spine. It passes the kidney, urinary bladderthen enters the abdomen, then the lungs, heart and pericardium. Another branch travels to the liverthen towards the base of the tongue.

As you meditate on this pathway, imagine being surrounded by water and charting nourishement to all the internal organs from the bottom of the foot up the throat.

Liver Meridian Medidation

This meridian begins at the big toeand moves along the top of the foot and up the legsto enter the groin area then ascendsinternally to the liver and gallbladder as well as dispersing along the ribs and chest as it moves towards the neck. It continues to the faceas it reaches the eyes. A branch circles the cheeks and lips then moves to the top of the head.

As you meditate on the course of this meridian, imagine being under a tree and release what you don't need into the earth as you continue to nourish the body along the course of the meridian.
